A missionary with a warm heart for people

Father Cândido Luhuma, born in Benguela, Angola, is a priest of the Congregation of the Holy Spirit (CSSp). His vocation began at a young age, inspired by missionaries in his native country. After his formation in the Netherlands, including in Gennep, Berg en Dal, and Handel, he was ordained a priest on October 16, 2016.

With his warm personality and commitment to people, he began his pastoral work at the Saint Willibrord Parish in Gemert-Bakel. There, he was active among young people, in catechesis, and in the local Brazilian community. His dedication was widely appreciated, and in 2024, he was transferred to our parish.

Father Cândido is also deeply involved with Catholic youth in the Netherlands. He is the coordinator for the youth apostolate in the Netherlands, is active in Alpha Netherlands, and is the founder of Soulfire (a Catholic youth group that meets twice a month).

Currently, Father Cândido is active in the Maria Magdalena federation, primarily serving the Lourdes Parish and St. Augustine Parish in Barendrecht, and assisting the Portuguese-speaking community in The Hague. He sees his calling as a lifelong commitment to be close to people and make God's love tangible—especially for young people and those seeking help.

Our History

The history of the Lourdes parish and the St. Bavo Church cannot be counted in years, but must be counted in centuries

For decades, the Church of Our Lady of Lourdes has been a familiar beacon of faith, hope, and community in the neighborhood. Built during a period of post-war reconstruction, the church has grown into a vibrant parish where people from diverse backgrounds gather around their faith.

The church is named after Mary of Lourdes, a symbol of comfort, prayer, and miracles. This spirit of closeness and care is still palpable in every celebration, meeting, and activity within our community. Over the years, the Lourdes Church has remained not only a place of worship, but also of solidarity, encounter, and inspiration – right in the heart of the city.

Today, we continue to build on this rich history, with an eye to the future and space for everyone who feels called to participate.
